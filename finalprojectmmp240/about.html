About

My name is Miriam and my story began on the southern shores of the Mediterranean, in a small Tunisian coastal town called Sidi Bou Said, where I enjoyed helping my grandma making her delightful pastries. We handpicked the most cherished ingredients such as pistachios, almonds, pine nuts, honey, and scented waters to make the mouth-watering Mlabes, Baklawa and Kaak.


Some told me that the Moors of Spain invented these little Gems. Others credit the Ottomans or even say it was the French who added the luxurious spin. My grandma told me that our pastries come from a mixture of cultures and civilizations, refined over the centuries. Everyone agrees that they hold a special place in the people's hearts around the Mediterranean.
